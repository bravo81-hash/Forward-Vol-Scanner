SMSF Playbook

Single-expiry structure selection for the cash account — SPX / RUT. The live selector is the SMSF tab in the app (Gate S); this page is the build reference behind each variant it can recommend.

Cash-account doctrine Single expiry only — calendars, diagonals and double calendars are blocked on EU cash-settled indices in a cash account, at initiation and as adjustments. Replicate what the time spread does: upside T+0 flattening → call debit vertical / ITM call / call BWB; positive vega → enter longer DTE (60–90); "buy time" → roll the tested wing or stack a second tent. All adjustments are debit-side — credit-spread adds consume cash equal to their full width.

02Build Specs

Put BWB

Default SMSF income engine — skew pays for the asymmetry
When Mid IV + steep put skew + neutral-to-slightly-bearish
DTE 60–80 entry; exit/roll by 30–35 DTE
Body Shorts 20–40 pts below spot (≈ −0.5σ to −1σ, ~30–35Δ)
Wings Upper 20–30 pts above body; lower broken 2–3× wider. Steeper skew ⇒ widen the broken wing for the same debit, or enter at a credit
Cash Lower-wing width × 100 − credit (or + debit)
Manage Target 8–12% of max risk. Debit-side adjustments only: call debit spread above the tent on a rally; stack a second BWB lower on a break

Put BWB + ITM Call (M3)

Single-expiry cousin of the fly + call calendar combo
When Mid IV + steep skew + mildly bullish bias
DTE 60–80, both components SAME expiry; exit by 30–35 DTE
Body BWB as standard; add 1 ITM call (70–80Δ) per 1–2 flies, strike ≈ 3–5% below spot
Wings The ITM call replaces the call calendar: upside T+0 flattening + positive delta
Cash Call debit adds; net position delta ≈ flat to +5 per lot
Manage Rally: call pays — roll it up (debit-neutral vertical roll). Selloff: call is the sacrifice, tent catches

Balanced ATM Put Fly

Symmetric carry when skew gives no subsidy
When Mid IV + flat skew + neutral
DTE 45–60; exit by 21 DTE or at target
Body Shorts at the money
Wings Symmetric 30–50 pts each side (SPX); ~1σ of remaining DTE
Cash One wing width × 100 − credit. Prefer all-put over iron fly: same graph, half the cash
Manage Target 10–15%; no repair doctrine — exit and re-centre

Iron Fly

Rich-premium harvest in high IV when skew is flat
When High IV + flat skew + pinned market
DTE 30–45 (shorter than the BWB book); exit fast
Body Shorts straddle ATM
Wings Wide — 1.25–1.5σ; high IV means wide wings still pay
Cash CAUTION: two credit spreads ⇒ cash held on BOTH widths. All-put balanced fly = same graph at half the cash
Manage Take 25% of credit quickly — these are IV-crush trades, not hold-to-expiry trades

OTM Put Fly (Wide)

High-IV income with a bearish tilt — profits from drift into the tent
When High IV + steep skew; also mid IV + bearish bias
DTE 45–60; exit by 21 DTE
Body Shorts at −1σ to −1.5σ below spot
Wings Wider than normal (vol-of-vol high): 40–60 pts SPX equivalent; size DOWN 25–50%
Cash Symmetric version in high IV — the broken wing adds tail cash use exactly when tails are live
Manage Target 8–10%, taken EARLY — IV crush does the work if price sits

Call BWB / Call Fly

Sell the upside when the call wing is the expensive side
When Call skew bid (rr25 negative) — post-crash rebounds, squeezes; more common in RUT
DTE 45–60
Body Shorts 1–3% above spot at the richest call strikes
Wings Lower wing near spot; upper wing broken wider above
Cash Broken-wing math mirrored from the put BWB
Manage Also the standing upside ADJUSTMENT: where the margin book adds a call diagonal, this book adds a small call BWB — similar T+0 reshaping, one expiry

Directional Target Fly

Cheap convexity to a specific level — a trade, not income
When Low IV with a directional view, or any strong view with a level
DTE Match the thesis horizon; 20–45 typical
Body Centred AT the target — call fly above for bullish, put fly below for bearish
Wings Narrow (10–25 pts SPX) — maximise reward-to-risk
Cash Debit only — cheapest structure in the book
Manage Lotto discipline: ≤ 0.25% NLV, no adjustments, take 100–200% of debit or let it die
